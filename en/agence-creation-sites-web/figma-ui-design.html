You approved beautiful mockups but the final site is disappointing. Spacing is off, colors don't match, interactions are missing. This gap between design and development is a classic problem. Frequently Asked Questions

EVERYTHING YOU NEED TO KNOW ABOUT UI/UX DESIGN IN FIGMA

What is the difference between UI and UX design? expand_more
UX (User Experience) concerns the overall user experience: how they navigate, how they find information, how they reach their goals on your website or application. It's the logic, the architecture, the journeys. UI (User Interface) concerns the visual aspect: typography, colors, buttons, icons, layout. The two disciplines are complementary and inseparable. A visually beautiful design that is hard to use will not convert. A smooth experienwhat looks mediocre will not inspire trust. We master both.
Why design in Figma rather than another tool? expand_more
Figma has become the industry standard for interface design, and for good reason. It is real-time collaborative — you can follow mockup progress live and leave comments directly on screens. It is cloud-based, so nothing to install and accessible from any browser. It offers native interactive prototyping features that let you test journeys without writing code. And above all, developers can inspect every element of the mockups and extract technical specifications (sizes, spacing, colors, typography) directly from Figma, guaranteeing a smooth handoff and a final result that is faithful to the design.
How much does a UI/UX design project cost? expand_more
A UI/UX design project for a 5-page showcase site starts att 1,200 euros. This includes wireframes, high-fidelity mockups in desktop and mobile versions, and the interactive prototype. For a more complex project such as a web application, e-commerce or SaaS, the budget ranges between 3,000 and 10,000 euros depending on the number of screens and the complexity of user journeys. A completee design system with component library is quoted separately (starting at 2,000 euros). Every quote is detailed and transparent.
What is a design system and do I need one? expand_more
A design system is an organized library of reusable visual components — buttons, forms, cards, menus, modals, tables, etc. — accompanied by typography, color, spacing and usage rules. It guarantees the visual consistency of your entire digital product and considerably speeds up the design of new pages or features. It is highly recommended for projects of more than 10 screens, SaaS products in constant evolution, or teams with multiple designers. For a simple showcase site it is not always necessary.
Do you deliver only the design or also the development? expand_more
We can do both. If you already have an internal development team or a technical partner, we deliver completee Figma mockups with technical specifications for a smooth handoff. The Figma file is organized with distinct pages for each screen, properly named components and an integrated style guide. If you need both design and development, we ensure completee continuity — the same teams that design your interfaces then build them on Webflow, WordPress, Shopify or custom development. This continuity guarantees pixel-perfect fidelity between the mockup and the final site.
How long does a UI/UX design project take? expand_more
A design project for a 5 to 7-page showcase site takes 1 to 2 weeks from brief to final mockup delivery. A web application or SaaS project with 15 to 30 screens takes 3 to 6 weeks. A completee design system with component library can take 4 to 8 weeks depending on the scope of the product. These timelines include research, wireframes, high-fidelity mockups, revisions and prototyping. We provide a precise schedule at the first meeting.
