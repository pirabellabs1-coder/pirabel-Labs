The problem

THE ABSENCE OF BRAND GUIDELINES IS COSTING YOU DEARLY

Without a clear reference document, your brand gets diluted a little more with every new visual. Here is what that causes.

shuffle

Every material has a different style

Your website uses certain colours, your Instagram posts use others, and your internal documents a third palette. The fonts change from one medium to another. The logo is used in different sizes and proportions. Your brand looks like a patchwork and your clients do not recognise it from one channel to the next.

timer

You waste time on every creation

Without established rules, every new visual requires starting from scratch. Which blue to use? Which font for the headings? What logo size? Your team spends more time asking these questions than producing content. And the result is rarely satisfying because everyone does it their own way.

warning

External providers do whatever they want

When you entrust a creation to a freelancer or an agency, the result never looks like your brand. Without a precise reference document, each provider interprets your identity their own way. You pay for endless corrections and the final output remains approximate.

trending_down

Your credibility takes a hit

Visual inconsistency sends a negative signal to your prospects: if you cannot control your own image, how could you control the quality of your products or services? Graphic confusion erodes trust before the first conversation even takes place.

Frequently Asked Questions

YOUR QUESTIONS ABOUT BRAND GUIDELINES

What is a brand guidelines exactly? expand_more
A brand guidelines is the reference document that gathers all the usage rules for your visual identity. It precisely defines how your logo, your colours, your typographies and your graphic elements must be used on each medium — website, social media, print, signage. It is the guardian of your brand consistency, the document that every person who creates a visual for you must consult.
Why do I need brand guidelines? expand_more
Without brand guidelines, every person who creates a material for your brand improvises according to their own interpretation. The result: inconsistent visuals that dilute your image and erode your clients' trust. The brand guidelines guarantees that your brand remains recognisable and professional, whether the visual is created by you, a collaborator, an intern or an external agency. It also saves you considerable time by eliminating back-and-forth feedback.
What does a complete brand guidelines contain? expand_more
A complete brand guidelines contains the logo usage rules with all its versions and graphic prohibitions, the complete colour palette with codes for web and print, the typographies with their hierarchy and usage rules, the layout and composition rules, the complementary graphic elements, the application examples on about ten real materials, and the editable templates for common materials.
How much does the creation of a brand guidelines cost? expand_more
A standalone brand guidelines, if you already have a logo and a defined visual identity, starts at 1,500 euros. As part of a complete visual identity including the logo, the guidelines are integrated into the overall price starting from 2,500 euros. The final price depends on the level of detail desired, the number of media covered and the complexity of your communication ecosystem.
Will my team be able to use the guidelines easily? expand_more
Yes, that is our absolute priority. Brand guidelines that nobody uses is useless guidelines. We design clear, visual and practical documents. Each rule is illustrated with concrete examples of what to do and what not to do. We also include editable templates on Canva, Figma, PowerPoint and Google Docs so that your team can create compliant materials without any design training.
What is the difference between a brand guidelines and a brand guide? expand_more
The brand guidelines focuses on strictly visual rules: logo, colours, typographies, layout, graphic elements. The brand guide goes further by including brand positioning, tone of voice (how you express yourself), communication strategy, guidelines for photography and video, and sometimes even values and mission. We offer both levels according to your needs and brand maturity.
