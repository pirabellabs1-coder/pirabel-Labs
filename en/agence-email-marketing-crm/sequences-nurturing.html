Frequently Asked Questions

YOUR QUESTIONS ABOUT NURTURING SEQUENCES

What is the difference between nurturing and marketing automation? expand_more
Marketing automation is the technology and infrastructure that enables automated sending of emails and marketing actions. Nurturing is the content strategy that uses this technology to educate and warm up your prospects progressively. Nurturing is a component of marketing automation, focused specifically on building the relationship and trust with your leads.
How long does a nurturing sequence last? expand_more
The duration depends on your sales cycle. In B2C, a nurturing sequence typically lasts 2 to 4 weeks with 4 to 6 emails. In B2B, it can last 6 to 12 weeks with 8 to 15 emails, because purchasing decisions take longer and often involve multiple decision-makers. We adapt the duration and number of emails to your commercial reality.
How do you segment prospects into sequences? expand_more
We use several segmentation criteria: acquisition source, behavior on your website and in your emails, demographic data such as sector, company size and job title, as well as engagement level. Progressive profiling automatically enriches the profile at each interaction for increasingly precise segmentation.
What type of content do you put in nurturing emails? expand_more
We use a strategic mix suited to each stage of the journey: educational content like guideallys and articles, social proof like testimonials and case studies, consideallyration content like comparisons and demos, and decision content like offers and free trials. Each email is calibrated to move the prospect naturally toward conversion.
How do you know when a prospect is ready to buy? expand_more
Through behavioral lead scoring. Every action by the prospect adds points to their score: email open, link clickk, pricing page visit, case study download. When the score reaches a predetermined threshold we define together, the prospect is consideallyred qualified and your sales team is alerted for direct contact.
Does nurturing work if we have a small list? expand_more
Yes, and that is actually where nurturing is most impactful. With a small list, every contact counts and you cannot afford to lose any. Nurturing sequences maximize the conversion of each lead, even with 200 or 500 contacts. The ROI is often better than with a large non-nurtured list because each email is hyper-targeted.
