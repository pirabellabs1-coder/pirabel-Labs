Frequently Asked Questions

EVERYTHING ABOUT MAKE AUTOMATION

What is Make and what is it used for? expand_more
Make (formerly Integromat) is a visual automation platform that lets you connect your applications together and create automated workflows without writing a single line of code. You can automate data transfers between tools, email sends, e-commerce order management, report creation and much more. Its drag-and-drop interface makes workflow creation intuitive, even for the most complex scenarios.
What is the difference between Make and Zapier? expand_more
Make offers a far more advanced visual interface than Zapier, with conditional logic, loops, routers and built-in error handling. Zapier is simpler for basic "if this then that" automations. Make is generally more powerful and more economical for complex workflows with multiple branches and conditions. We recommend the tool best suited to your specific needs.
How many integrations are available on Make? expand_more
Make offers more than 1500 native integrations with the most popular applications: Google Workspace, Slack, HubSpot, Shopify, WooCommerce, Stripe, Airtable, Notion, Mailchimp and hundreds more. For applications that do not have a native integration, Make lets you create connections via HTTP, webhooks and REST API. You can therefore connect virtually any tool to your automation system.
How much does it cost to implement a Make automation? expand_more
Our Starter package starts at 800 euros for 3 automated workflows and the connection of 5 tools. The Growth package at 2,000 euros covers unlimited workflows with advanced integrations and an AI chatbot. For complex systems, we offer custom Enterprise solutions. On top of this is the cost of the Make subscription itself (starting from 9 euros per month). The return on investment is visible within the first month.
Can Make handle complex automations? expand_more
Absolutely. Make excels at complex workflows. It has iterators to handle data lists, aggregators to consolidate information, routers to create multiple conditional branches and error handling modules to guarantee reliability. A single Make scenario can contain dozens of steps with multiple branches and sophisticated error handling. It is the ideal tool for advanced automations.
How quickly will my Make automations be operational? expand_more
A simple workflow — for example an automatic data transfer between two tools — is operational in 3 to 5 days. A complete system with several interconnected workflows, advanced conditional logic and multiple integrations takes 2 to 4 weeks. We proceed step by step: each workflow is tested and validated under real conditions before moving to the next. You have complete visibility on project progress.
