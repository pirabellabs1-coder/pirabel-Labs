The problem

THESE MISTAKES MAKE YOUR ARTICLES INVISIBLE

Your blog has potential, but these common mistakes prevent your articles from reaching the first page of Google.

search_off

You write without keyword research

You choose your topics by gut feeling, without checking what your audience actually searches for on Google. Result: your articles match no search query and remain invisible in the results. Writing a good article without keyword research is like opening a shop with no sign.

format_align_left

Your article structure is random

Your headings follow no logical hierarchy, subheadings include no strategic keywords, and the structure facilitates neither reading nor crawling by Google. A poorly structured article loses rankings to competitors who master the SEO playbook.

link_off

No internal linking between your content

Your articles are isolated islands. No links connect them or guide the reader toward your conversion pages. Without internal linking, Google does not understand your site structure and does not properly distribute SEO authority between your pages.

trending_flat

Your articles attract but do not convert

Even when your articles generate traffic, visitors leave without ever becoming leads or clients. No clear call to action, no lead magnet, no conversion path. Your blog attracts people but does not fill your sales pipeline.

Frequently Asked Questions

YOUR QUESTIONS ABOUT OUR SEO BLOG ARTICLES

How long does it take for an SEO article to rank on Google? expand_more
A well-optimized article starts generating organic traffic between 4 and 8 weeks after publication. Rankings improve progressively over 3 to 6 months thanks to the accumulation of positive signals: time on page, shares, natural backlinks. The major advantage of SEO content: an article continues to bring you traffic for months, even years, unlike advertising that stops the moment you cut the budget.
What is the ideal length for an SEO article? expand_more
Our articles generally range between 1500 and 3000 words. The optimal length depends on the competition for the target keyword and the user's search intent. A complex topic like a comprehensive guide requires an in-depth article, while a simple question deserves a direct and concise answer. We systematically analyze the articles already ranking on the SERP before determining the ideal length for each topic.
How do you choose which keywords to target? expand_more
We cross-reference three fundamental criteria: search volume (how many people search for this keyword every month), ranking difficulty (how much effort it will take to rank), and commercial intent (are the people searching this keyword likely to become your clients). We use tools like Ahrefs, SEMrush, and Google Keyword Planner to identify the best opportunities specific to your industry.
Do you write the articles or is it AI? expand_more
Every article is written by a human expert writer in your field. We use AI as a research and ideation tool, but the writing, editing, and validation are 100% human. Google values E-E-A-T content (Experience, Expertise, Authority, Trustworthiness) and penalizes content generated en masse by AI. That is why we invest in editorial quality rather than automated production.
How many articles should be published per month to get results? expand_more
Quality always trumps quantity. Generally, 4 to 8 strategic articles per month are enough to build a solid organic presence. What matters is that every article answers a precise search intent, targets a strategic keyword, and fits within a coherent semantic cluster. A single well-optimized article that ranks on the first page is worth infinitely more than 20 generic articles that nobody reads.
What exactly does the delivery of an SEO article include? expand_more
Each delivered article includes: validated keyword research, optimized structure with H1 to H4 heading hierarchy, content written and edited by an expert, optimized meta title and meta description for clicks, internal linking suggestions toward your key pages, alt tags for images, integrated calls to action, and two rounds of revisions. You receive complete content, ready to publish in your CMS.
