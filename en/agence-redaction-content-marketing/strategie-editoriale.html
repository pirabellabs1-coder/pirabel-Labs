The problem

WITHOUT A STRATEGY, YOUR CONTENT IS WASTED EFFORT

You may have talent for writing, but without a strategy, your efforts produce no measurable result.

shuffle

You publish randomly, without a plan or coherence

An article on SEO Monday, a post on productivity Wednesday, an email on finance Friday. Your content goes in every direction. Without defined content pillars, your blog looks like a patchwork with no identity. Your audience does not know what to expect and Google does not know how to classify you.

person_off

You do not know who you are speaking to

You write for everyone, therefore for no one. Without clearly defined personas, your content resonates with no specific audience. It is too generic to capture the attention of your ideal clients and too vague to answer their real questions.

event_busy

You have no publication calendar

Every week, it is the same question: what to publish? You improvise, you postpone, you end up publishing nothing at all for weeks. Without an editorial calendar, you lose the consistency that is essential to building an audience and pleasing Google.

analytics

You measure nothing and learn from nothing

You publish content but you do not know if it works. No defined KPIs, no performance tracking, no optimization. You repeat the same mistakes and miss opportunities that your data could reveal.

Frequently Asked Questions

YOUR QUESTIONS ABOUT EDITORIAL STRATEGY

What is an editorial strategy exactly? expand_more
An editorial strategy is the master plan for all your content. It precisely defines who you speak to (detailed personas), what you say (content pillars and semantic clusters), how you say it (brand voice and tone), when you publish (editorial calendar), and how you measure results (KPIs). It is the fundamental difference between publishing content randomly and methodically building a traffic and lead generation machine.
How long does it take to build an editorial strategy? expand_more
Allow 2 to 4 weeks for a complete editorial strategy. The first week is dedicated to auditing your existing content and competitive research. The second to persona definition and brand voice. The following weeks to the editorial calendar and finalizing deliverables. You are involved at every validation step to ensure the strategy matches your vision.
What does the editorial strategy deliverable contain? expand_more
You receive a complete operational document including: the detailed audit of your existing content with recommendations, persona profiles (3 to 5 personas with demographics, pain points, goals), the brand voice charter with examples, content pillars and semantic clusters, the editorial calendar over 3 to 12 months with each topic, target keyword, format, and objective, and the KPI tracking dashboard.
Is it necessary to have existing content to get started? expand_more
Not at all. We build editorial strategies for businesses starting from zero as well as for those that already have hundreds of articles to optimize. If you have existing content, we begin with a complete audit that identifies what works and what needs improvement. If you are starting from scratch, we build the strategy based on analysis of your market, your competitors, and your business objectives.
How often is the editorial calendar updated? expand_more
The initial calendar covers 3 to 12 months depending on your plan. It is reviewed and adjusted every month based on the performance of published content, emerging trends, and newly identified SEO opportunities. Audience feedback, analytics data, and market evolution feed these regular updates. The calendar is a living document that adapts permanently.
Do you also execute the strategy or just design it? expand_more
Both options are available. We can design the strategy and train you to execute it internally with your teams, or take charge of full execution: article writing, content creation, publication in your CMS, and performance tracking. Most of our clients choose the full support package because it guarantees consistency and quality over time. You keep full control over validation.
