Frequently Asked Questions

YOUR QUESTIONS ABOUT NETLINKING

What is link building and why is it important for SEO? expand_more
Link building consists of obtaining hyperlinks from other websites to yours. Google considers every inbound link as a vote of confidence in your website. The more links you accumulate from credible, relevant sites, the more Google deems your content trustworthy and worthy of ranking highly in the search results. It is one of the three fundamental pillars of SEO, alongside content and technical optimization.
Is link building risky for my site? expand_more
Poorly executed link building is indeed risky. Bulk buying of low-quality links, links from spam sites and over-optimization of anchor texts can trigger a manual or algorithmic Google penalty. Our approach is radically different: we exclusively use ethical methods approved by Google — press relations, quality content creation, authentic editorial partnerships. Across more than 2500 links acquired, we have never caused a single penalty.
How many backlinks are needed to see results? expand_more
The answer depends entirely on your sector and the level of competition. In general, 5 to 15 quality links per month is a good pace to see significant progress within 2 to 4 months. The key point to remember is that a single link from a high-authority site (a national media outlet, an industry reference site) can be worth as much as 50 links from small sites. Quality always and systematically outweighs quantity.
How do you obtain quality backlinks? expand_more
We use several complementary methods. Digital press relations allow us to obtain links from media outlets and news sites. Guest blogging opens the doors of influential thematic blogs. The creation of linkable content (studies, infographics, tools) attracts links naturally. Editorial partnerships with players in your ecosystem generate organic mentions. Each method is suited to your sector and your objectives.
What is the difference between a good and a bad backlink? expand_more
A good backlink comes from a real website with genuine traffic, relevant to your topic, and with good domain authority. It is placed in a natural editorial context, within quality content. In contrast, a bad backlink comes from a spam site, a low-quality directory, a network of sites created solely to sell links, or an automated blog comment. We only work with links whose quality is verifiable and verified.
Can I see which links you get for my site? expand_more
Absolutely, and it is even a central element of our method. Transparency is non-negotiable. Every month, you receive a report detailing every link obtained: the source page URL, the site name, its domain authority, the anchor text used, the placement context and the acquisition date. You can verify every link yourself at any time. No link is placed in the dark.
