The problem

THESE TECHNICAL PROBLEMS THAT SABOTAGE YOUR SEO

Your website may be beautiful and have excellent content. If these technical problems exist, Google will never show you on the first page.

speed

Your website is too slow

Every extra second of loading time drives away 10% of your visitors. Google penalizes slow sites in its rankings. If your website takes more than 3 seconds to load, you are losing clients and rankings.

bug_report

Errors are blocking indexation

404 errors, redirect loops, misconfigured robots.txt, missing sitemap. These problems prevent Google from accessing your pages. Pages that Google cannot see are pages that do not exist.

phone_android

Your website is not mobile-friendly

Over 60% of searches are done on mobile. Google uses the mobile version of your website to rank it. If your website is difficult to use on phone, you are penalized on all searches.

code_off

Markup is missing or incorrect

Without structured markup (Schema.org, Hn tags, meta descriptions), Google does not understand the content of your pages. You miss out on rich snippets and featured positions that capture user attention.

Frequently Asked Questions

YOUR QUESTIONS ABOUT TECHNICAL SEO

What exactly is technical SEO? expand_more
Technical SEO encompasses all the optimizations that allow Google to find, understand, and correctly index your website. This includes loading speed, site architecture, HTML markup, mobile compatibility, and indexation management. It is the foundation on which the rest of your SEO strategy is built.
Why is my site slow and how does that affect my SEO? expand_more
A slow site drives visitors away and directly penalizes your Google rankings. Common causes include oversized images, unoptimized code, poor hosting, or too many external scripts. Google measures speed via Core Web Vitals and uses it as a ranking factor. If your website takes more than 3 seconds to load, you lose more than half of your mobile visitors.
What are Core Web Vitals? expand_more
Core Web Vitals are three key metrics defined by Google to measure user experience quality. LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) measures the display speed of main content. INP (Interaction to Next Paint) measures site responsiveness when users click or type. CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift) measures the visual stability of the page during loading. Google has used them as a ranking factor since 2021.
How long does a technical optimization take? expand_more
A completee technical audit takes 5 to 10 business days depending on the size of your website. Priority fixes are deployed within 2 to 4 weeks. The first results in terms of rankings are generally visible within 4 to 8 weeks after the fixes. Some gains, like speed improvements, are immediate for your users.
Is technical SEO enough to be first on Google? expand_more
Technical SEO is the absolutely essential foundation, but it is not enough on its own. To reach the first page, you also need quality content and relevant backlinks. However, without a solid technical foundation, even the best content in the world will not perform. That is why we always recommend starting with the technical side.
My site was recently redesigned, do I need technical SEO? expand_more
Yes, and it is actually an urgent situation. The vast majority of website redesigns introduce SEO errors without anyone realizing it: missing redirects, orphan pages, incorrect markup, loss of internal linking, URL changes without redirects. A post-redesign technical audit is essential to avoid a sharp drop in your organic traffic.
