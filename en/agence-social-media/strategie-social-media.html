If you recognize yourself in any of these situations, your social presence needs a strategic direction.

Frequently Asked Questions

YOUR QUESTIONS ABOUT SOCIAL MEDIA STRATEGY

Why do you need a social media strategy? expand_more
Posting without a strategy is like driving without GPS. You spend time and energy without knowing if you are heading in the right direction. A social media strategy defines your objectives, target audiences, key messages and editorial calendar. Each post has a precise purpose, and you can measure what works and adjust continuously.
How long does it take to develop a social media strategy? expand_more
The full development of a social media strategy takes between 2 and 3 weeks. This includes the audit of your current presence, competitor analysis, persona definition, editorial calendar creation and validation with your team. It is a time investment that determines the success of everything that follows.
How many platforms should you be present on? expand_more
It is better to excel on 2 or 3 platforms than to be mediocre on 5. We analyze your target audience, your sector and your resources to recommend the most profitable platforms. For visual B2C, Instagram and TikTok are priorities. For B2B, LinkedIn is essential. Facebook remains relevant for local communities.
How often should you post on social media? expand_more
The ideal frequency depends on the platform and your resources. In general, we recommend 3 to 5 posts per week on Instagram, 1 to 2 per day on LinkedIn, 3 to 7 videos per week on TikTok. Consistency is more important than quantity: 3 excellent posts per week are better than 7 mediocre ones.
How do you measure the success of a social media strategy? expand_more
We define KPIs aligned with your business objectives: engagement rate, community growth, generated traffic, qualified leads, conversions and attributable revenue. Each month, we measure these indicators and adjust the strategy to improve results. No vanity metrics, only the numbers that matter.
Is the strategy adapted if our objectives change? expand_more
Absolutely. A social media strategy is not a fixed document. We review it each quarter and adjust it based on your new objectives, market trends and observed performance. If you launch a new product, target a new market or change positioning, we adapt the strategy accordingly.
