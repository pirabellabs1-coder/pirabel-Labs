The problem

DOES THIS SOUND FAMILIAR?

If you recognize yourself in any of these situations, you're losing money every day.

traffic

Traffic, but zero conversion

You have visitors. You might even have many of them. But they arrive on your site and leave without doing anything. No purchase, no form filled, no contact. Your traffic costs money, but it brings in nothing. It's like having a store full of people where no one goes to the register.

exit_to_app

Skyrocketing bounce rate

Your visitors arrive and leave your site in a few seconds. Your home page or your service pages hold no one. The message is not clear, the value proposition is drowned out, and the call to action is invisible. Each bounce is a potential customer you'll never see again.

remove_shopping_cart

Massive cart abandonment

Your customers add products to the cart, start the buying process... and disappear. Your checkout is too long, too complex, or doesn't inspire confidence. Each abandoned cart represents revenue that escapes you when the customer was ready to pay.

edit_off

Forms no one fills out

Your contact or quote form remains desperately empty. Too many fields, a design that doesn't inspire confidence, or simply poorly placed on the page. Your prospects want to contact you, but your form discourages them even before they start filling it out.

hourglass_top

Slow loading pages

Each extra second of loading scares away 7% of your visitors. Your landing page takes 4 seconds to display? You've already lost a quarter of your audience before they even see your offer. Speed is not a technical detail — it's a direct conversion lever.

route

No structured customer journey

Your visitors arrive on your site without knowing where to go. No sales funnel, no logical sequence, no follow-up. You hope they find the path to purchase on their own. Without a customer journey, you let chance decide your conversions.

Our approach

Frequently asked questions

EVERYTHING YOU WANT TO KNOW ABOUT CRO

What is CRO and why is it important? expand_more
CRO (Conversion Rate Optimization) consists of improving the percentage of visitors who perform a desired action on your site: purchase, registration, request for quote. It's important because doubling your conversion rate is equivalent to doubling your turnover without spending a single extra euro on advertising. You get more value from the traffic you already have.
How long does it take to see results with CRO? expand_more
The first results of A/B testing are visible in 2 to 4 weeks, depending on your traffic volume. A complete CRO strategy shows significant gains within 2 to 3 months. The advantage of CRO over SEO is that improvements are immediate: as soon as a test is a winner, you benefit from the gain on 100% of your traffic.
What is the difference between a landing page and a classic page? expand_more
A landing page is designed with a single conversion goal: no distracting navigation menu, no outgoing links, a clear message and a unique call to action. A classic page has several goals and offers many navigation options. It is this extreme focus that makes landing pages much more effective — on average 2 to 5 times more conversions than a standard page.
How does A/B testing work? expand_more
A/B testing consists of creating two versions of a page or an element (title, button, image, form) and sending half of the traffic to each. We measure which converts best with statistically reliable data. When we have a clear winner, we deploy the best version and launch a new test. It's a cycle of continuous improvement based on evidence, not opinions.
What is a sales funnel? expand_more
A sales funnel is a structured journey that guides your visitors step by step towards purchase. Instead of letting them navigate randomly on your site, you accompany them with content, offers and follow-ups adapted to each stage of their thinking: discovery, consideration, decision. Result: more conversions, a higher average cart, and more satisfied customers.
What budget do I need to start with CRO? expand_more
Our Starter formula at 800 euros per month is ideal for starting. It includes the conversion audit, two optimized landing pages and a first A/B testing cycle. The return on investment is often visible from the first month thanks to immediate conversion gains. Unlike SEO which takes time, CRO generates results quickly.
Does CRO work for small businesses? expand_more
Absolutely. CRO is even more impactful for small businesses because each visitor counts more. If you receive 500 visitors per month and you go from 1% to 3% conversion, you triple your customers without increasing your marketing budget. It's one of the most profitable levers for companies with a limited budget.
What tools do you use for CRO? expand_more
We use professional tools like Google Analytics 4 for data analysis, Hotjar for heatmaps and session recordings, VWO for A/B testing, as well as advanced tracking solutions for multi-channel attribution. All tools are configured to respect GDPR and protect your visitors' data.
